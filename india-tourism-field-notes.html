when to go

Winter wins, every single year

Traffic climbs through autumn, peaks October–January, then falls off hard once the heat arrives. The pattern repeats so reliably across the decade that "month" is a more useful predictor than "year" ever was.

    who's actually visiting

    This is a domestic story, not a global one

    Nine or ten out of every ten visitors are domestic travelers. The decade's growth curve is almost entirely a story about more Indians visiting their own heritage sites — foreign arrivals stayed comparatively flat.

    field notes

    What this actually means

    Plan for the superstars

    Crowd control, ticketing capacity, and infrastructure spend should follow the Pareto curve — a small set of sites will always absorb most footfall, regardless of national campaigns.

    Use the calendar, not the crystal ball

    The model barely uses "year" to predict traffic. Season is a far more reliable planning lever than trying to forecast growth.

    Foreign growth needs a different play

    Since organic growth is domestic-driven, reaching international travelers likely requires deliberately marketing the "foreign favorite" sites, not the already-saturated top domestic draws.

    There's more signal to add

    Weather, public holidays, and event calendars could sharpen the model further — Year and Month alone already carry real signal; local context would add more.
