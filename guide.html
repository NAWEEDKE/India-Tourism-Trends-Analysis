1. Introduction & Overview

Welcome to the modernized India Tourism Field Notes Dashboard. This application presents a comprehensive 10-year monthly visitor analysis (2010 to 2019) across historical heritage sites in India. By combining structured dataset stats with custom architectural sketches, the dashboard offers an organic, hand-crafted "notebook" aesthetic for exploring tourism data.

2. Navigation Manual

  • Page Index Tabs: Use the right-edge indexing tabs (Overview, Seasonality, Monuments, Circles, Guide) to flip through different sections of the notebook.
  • Specimen Details: Navigate to the Monuments page. Click on any monument card to slide open its detail card popup. This popup displays a clean hand-drawn sketch, construction period, commissioner/builder, architectural style, and specific field annotations.
  • Heatmap cells: In the Seasonality page, hover your cursor over any calendar grid cell to read the exact domestic visitor count recorded for that month/year combination.

3. Core Dataset Insights

  • Monument Dominance: Predictive modeling reveals that which monument a site is accounts for 73.1% of visitor volumes. Circular regions account for 18.5%, seasonality 5.6%, and year-over-year growth accounts for just 2.6%.
  • Extreme Climate Impact: Monthly seasonality averages indicate a 4x reduction in visitors during high-temperature summer months (April-June) compared to winter months (December-February).
  • Domestic and Foreign Ratios: Local Indian visitors constitute 94% of overall national ticket sales. However, foreign tourism is highly concentrated in key architectural landmarks, such as the Taj Mahal and Humayun's Tomb.

4. Data Dictionary

Parameter Description
Domestic Local Indian citizens visiting cultural heritage monuments.
Foreign International travelers/tourists visiting from overseas.
Circle Regional administrative division overseen by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI).
